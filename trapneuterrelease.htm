According to Houston City Ordinance section 6-22, "It shall be unlawful for any person intentionally to cause, suffer, or permit the maintenance of an attractive environment for the assembly of a congregation of unconfined and unlicensed stray cats or dogs by the placement of dog food or cat food," however colony managers who follow city TNR guidelines are permitted to feed community cats "subject to a trap, neuter, and return program approved by the Director."

Why The Maxie Street Colony was Registered

The Maxie Street Cat Colony was established a long time ago. However, it was only recently registered with the City of Houston through the BARC program in 2013. There was a genuine desire to help the cats of the community through feeding them and helping to stop the many litters of kittens. Once it was realized that only cat colony managers could legally feed the community cats, the forms were filled out to become managers. Furthermore, once involved in the process of being a colony manager, the many benefits to the cats as well as the community became clearer. Not only were the cats that were part of our neighborhood healthier, they had increased chances of living longer happier lives. By having all the members spayed or neutered, a conscious decision was made to help in a humane and effective way to do this small part of helping to control the over population of community cats in our neighborhood.

Register to Become a Cat Colony Manager

If you are feeding community cats in your neighborhood, you should register to become a cat colony manager. The process is not a difficult one and is fully explained on BARC’s Get Involved page. From there you will find the requirements and the forms needed to apply to become a cat colony manager.

Make a Donation

If you do not wish to become a cat colony manager, but would still like to support the work of BARC, you can make a donation. Donations can include:

  • Volunteering your time
  • Fostering homeless animals
  • Dog and cat treats
  • Dawn dishwashing liquid
  • Kong toys and other washable dog toys
  • And a long list of other things found on the site.

Your donations helps BARC continue to evolve their shelter operations, and grow their adoption and foster programs. Every dollar, every gift and donation that you give, helps to increase the chances that animals at BARC will find permanent homes with loving families.
